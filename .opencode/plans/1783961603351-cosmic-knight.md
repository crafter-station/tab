# Tab Brand Foundation Plan

## Objective

Create `docs/brand.md` as the strategic and visual foundation for a future abstract, minimal SVG logo. This phase defines what the mark must communicate and how concepts will be judged; it does not design or integrate the logo itself.

The document will center the brand on:

> **A small, deliberate continuation.**

This captures the chosen priorities: effortless continuation first, quiet precision in personality, privacy through control, intelligence without spectacle, and speed without frantic motion.

## Source Material

Keep the new document aligned with these existing sources of truth:

- `CONTEXT.md`: canonical product language and rejected synonyms.
- `docs/PRD.md`: product behavior, trust boundaries, and qualified claims.
- `docs/product-strategy.md`: audience and local-first product promise.
- `docs/design-system.md`: the quiet native utility direction and orange product accent.
- `packages/ui/src/styles/globals.css`: implemented warm-neutral palette, burnt orange, typography, and light/dark modes.
- `packages/ui/src/components/app/patterns.tsx`: current provisional `TabMark` and its 32px usage.

## Document Structure

### 1. Purpose And Status

- Identify the document as a logo-foundation brief, not a complete brand manual.
- State that product terminology remains governed by `CONTEXT.md` and UI tokens by `docs/design-system.md`.
- Mark the current letter-in-a-square glyph as provisional rather than treating it as a constraint on the future mark.

### 2. Product And Audience

- Define Tab as a macOS-native autocomplete utility that offers short Suggestions in the applications where people already write.
- Name the initial audience: Mac-based developers and technical founders who move among Slack, email, Linear, GitHub, terminals, and AI coding tools.
- Clarify what Tab is not: a chatbot, destination writing app, browser extension, autonomous author, keylogger, or generic AI assistant.

### 3. Brand Core

- Brand essence: **A small, deliberate continuation.**
- Product promise: autocomplete across the Mac, private by default and smarter when the user asks.
- Emotional outcome: thought and typing keep moving together without surrendering control.
- Organize the requested qualities by role rather than treating them as four equal symbols:
  - **Tab / continuation:** the primary idea; a short next step in an existing flow.
  - **Private:** local-first processing, bounded context, visibility, consent, and deliberate Acceptance.
  - **Smart:** relevant, context-aware help that remains subordinate to the writer.
  - **Fast:** low-friction rhythm and immediacy, not a broad or unqualified latency claim.

### 4. Personality And Tensions

- Define the personality as quiet, precise, capable, respectful, and developer-fluent.
- Add paired guardrails that make the desired tone actionable:
  - fast, not frantic;
  - smart, not magical;
  - private, not defensive;
  - helpful, not autonomous;
  - native, not an imitation of Apple branding;
  - abstract, not arbitrary.

### 5. Logo Design Objective

- State the perceptual goal: make the next step feel immediate, controlled, and effortless.
- Prioritize recognition in this order: continuation, control, quiet intelligence, speed.
- Do not require a literal `T`; a latent or emergent `T` may be explored only when it strengthens the core idea.
- Treat the symbol as the primary design task and the `Tab` wordmark as a later typography/lockup task.

### 6. Visual Principles

- Use one strong silhouette with minimal geometry and intentional negative space.
- Suggest forward continuation through extension, transition, a gap, a junction, or a bounded opening rather than arrows or speed lines.
- Express privacy through agency and boundaries rather than locks, shields, eyes, fingerprints, or surveillance imagery.
- Express intelligence through fit, alignment, or an unexpectedly resolved relationship between forms rather than brains, nodes, circuits, sparkles, or magic wands.
- Keep the geometry calm and exact: no ornamental details, fake depth, glow, glass, gradients, or decorative motion.
- Design in monochrome first. Use the existing burnt orange (`#c83f00`) as an optional recognition accent, never as the feature that makes the mark legible.
- Ensure equivalent light-background, dark-background, and `currentColor` treatments.

### 7. Concept Territories For Exploration

Define territories as prompts for later sketching, not predetermined logo solutions:

1. **The Continuation Gap:** one form resolves or extends another across a deliberate interval; emphasizes the next few words and Acceptance.
2. **The Guided Advance:** a compact form changes state or moves forward along a precise path; emphasizes flow and speed without aggression.
3. **The Local Boundary:** a continuation begins inside a contained space and opens only at a chosen point; supports the privacy/control story without literal security symbols.

Recommend beginning with the Continuation Gap and allowing supporting ideas from the other territories to appear only when the mark stays simple.

### 8. Explicit Avoid List

- Browser tabs or browser chrome.
- Literal keyboard keys as the whole idea.
- Generic AI sparkles, brains, neural nodes, circuit traces, chat bubbles, and robots.
- Locks, shields, eyes, fingerprints, keyholes, and surveillance motifs.
- Lightning bolts, chevrons, arrows, wings, and speed streaks used as shorthand for performance.
- Clouds as the dominant symbol, because local inference is the product's center of gravity.
- Apple silhouettes or macOS icon mimicry.
- A standalone generic `T` monogram without a second, product-specific idea.

### 9. Production Constraints

- True SVG geometry, not embedded raster art or outlined text.
- Clear at 16px for favicon and macOS menu-bar use, with no thin details that disappear.
- Strong at the current 32px web and desktop lockup size.
- Effective as a single-color silhouette and when inverted.
- Compatible with a square app-icon composition without requiring the primary mark itself to be a rounded square.
- Balanced on warm-light and green-black/dark surfaces.
- No dependency on animation, gradient, shadow, or multiple colors for recognition.
- Simple enough to derive template/raster assets later for macOS tray, favicon, app icon, social, and web contexts.

### 10. Evaluation Scorecard

Include a practical concept-review matrix scored from 1 to 5:

- Communicates continuation before security or AI.
- Feels quiet and precise.
- Suggests user control without literal privacy iconography.
- Feels quick without looking aggressive or unstable.
- Is distinctive from generic productivity and AI marks.
- Avoids browser-tab confusion.
- Reads at 16px in one color.
- Works on light and dark backgrounds.
- Can pair naturally with the short `Tab` wordmark.
- Remains understandable without an explanatory animation.

Require concepts to pass the monochrome 16px test and avoid all explicit anti-patterns before subjective preference is considered.

### 11. Next-Phase Deliverables

Record the intended follow-up without producing it in this phase:

- Sketch multiple black-and-white SVG concepts in the primary territory.
- Test silhouettes at 16px and 32px before adding color.
- Compare finalists with the scorecard.
- Refine one mark, then create light, dark, `currentColor`, wordmark lockup, favicon, tray, and app-icon derivatives.

## Files To Modify

## Verification

1. Review every product term against `CONTEXT.md`; use `Suggestion`, `Acceptance`, `Typing Context`, `Local Suggestion`, and `Deep Complete` precisely.
2. Check claims against `docs/PRD.md` and `docs/product-strategy.md`; avoid promises such as universal app support, always-instant performance, total locality, or autonomous intelligence.
3. Confirm the palette and personality guidance agrees with `docs/design-system.md` and `packages/ui/src/styles/globals.css`: warm-neutral foundation, orange as accent, light/dark support, quiet native utility.
4. Confirm the document gives a future designer enough direction to reject weak concepts without prescribing one finished symbol.
5. Run `node --test tests/prd-contracts.test.mjs` to catch source-of-truth or terminology regressions, then inspect the Markdown diff for structure, links, and accidental scope creep.
