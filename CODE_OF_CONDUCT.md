# Code of Conduct

Tab should be a practical, welcoming place to contribute. We expect everyone in the project to help keep discussions useful, respectful, and focused on the work.

## Expected Behavior

- Be respectful of different experience levels, backgrounds, and constraints.
- Give actionable feedback. Prefer specific examples over broad judgments.
- Assume good intent, but be direct about bugs, safety risks, and privacy concerns.
- Keep disagreements technical and product-focused.
- Credit other people's work and ideas when building on them.
- Make space for first-time contributors by explaining project context and tradeoffs.

## Unacceptable Behavior

- Harassment, intimidation, threats, or sustained disruption.
- Demeaning comments about a person or group.
- Sexualized language or attention.
- Publishing private information without consent.
- Sharing secrets, private user data, raw typing logs, credentials, or other sensitive material.
- Repeatedly ignoring maintainer direction about scope, safety, or conduct.

## Reporting

If you experience or observe unacceptable behavior, contact a maintainer privately when possible. If you do not have a private channel, open a minimal public issue asking for a maintainer contact and do not include sensitive personal details.

Maintainers may remove comments, close issues or pull requests, block accounts, or take other action needed to protect contributors and users.

## Scope

This code of conduct applies in repository issues, pull requests, discussions, reviews, community spaces, and any project-related interaction.
